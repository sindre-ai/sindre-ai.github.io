"The process was never the product. It was the cost of coordination. And we were optimising the cost, not the outcome."

For fifty years, every methodology we built was designed to protect expensive engineering time. Waterfall, Agile, Scrum, SAFe — all sophisticated answers to the same question: how do we coordinate scarce, expensive humans around a long-running build?

That constraint is dissolving. Agents can carry the standups, the tickets, the handoffs, the status updates, the documentation, the chasing. The coordination overhead that consumed most of a team's energy can now run itself.

What's left is what was always the actual work: judgment, creative leaps, relationships, and craft. The taste to know which bet is worth making. The conversation that surfaces what a customer actually needs. The instinct to throw out a draft and start again. These don't compress, and they don't delegate. They're the product.
