What we believe in

For fifty years, every methodology we built answered the same question: how do you protect expensive engineering time from being wasted? Waterfall. Agile. Scrum. SAFe. OKRs. They were all coordination tools. And someone had to manage all of it. Product managers became the integration layer between customer and engineer, because nothing else could be.

We got good at it. So good we forgot: the process was never the product. It was the cost of coordination. And we were optimising the cost, not the outcome.

Now that cost is collapsing. What took a sprint takes an afternoon. What required three engineers requires one with an agent. The scarcity that justified every ticket, every standup, every backlog grooming session is gone.

The instinct is to use AI to do the old job faster. Generate the roadmap. Write the PRD. Summarise the standup. It feels productive. It is a trap. Accelerating the overhead is not escaping it. The opportunity was never speed. It is replacement.

Strip away the process and what remains is the work people were always supposed to do. Judgment: which bet is worth making. Creative leaps: the insight that connects signals no one else saw. Relationships: the trust that closes the deal. Craft: the quality bar that separates good enough from great.

Agents carry the process. Humans carry the product. That is the future we are building.

Real agents. Real work. One decision from you.

A customer signal arrives. The Insight Scout catches it, enriches it, links it to a cluster of related signals. The Bet Shepherd proposes an experiment: two weeks, a clear success metric. You review, accept. One decision. Tasks decompose automatically. The agent executes.
The Pipeline Scout spots a pattern: one ICP segment converts at 3x the average, but outreach is flat. The Growth Strategist proposes a campaign. The SDR Agent drafts personalised messages, researched, unique, queued for your review. You approve. The rest runs.
Import your contacts. The SDR Agent researches each one, drafts outreach, times the follow-up. You review what matters. Adjust one message. Close the deal yourself, because that part was always yours.

What makes Maskin different

Humans and agents as equals

Same API surface. Same workspace. Agents are not tools you pick up and put down. They are digital colleagues with persistent memory, defined roles, and ongoing responsibilities. You decide. They execute. Everyone sees the same data.

Extensible by design

Three primitives: Insights, Bets, Tasks. They mirror the scientific method: observe, hypothesise, experiment. Add your own object types on top. The same system composes differently for every team.

Secure and sandboxed

Agents run in parallel, each safely contained. Built with security in mind. Inspect every layer, run on your own infrastructure, bring your own models. Trust is not optional.

Built for builders

MCP-native

Every agent speaks MCP. Import integrations, connect tools, bring your own model.

Knowledge graph

Objects, relationships, and extensions. Not another database with a chat wrapper.

Triggers and automation

Fire agents on schedules or state changes. The machine runs while you sleep.

Open source

Inspect every layer. Fork it. Extend it. Ship it on your infra.
